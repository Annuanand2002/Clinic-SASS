How inventory works

Inventory tracks what you stock, how much you have per batch, and every stock in or out. Use Register for day-to-day actions and Report for alerts and overview.

1. Items (catalog)

Each item has a name, category (consumable, medicine, equipment), unit (e.g. box, pcs), and minimum stock. When total on hand falls below that level, it appears as a low-stock alert on the Report page and is highlighted in Stock view.

2. Add stock (purchase)

Receiving goods creates a batch line (quantity, optional batch number, purchase date, supplier, price, expiry) and records an IN movement. Stock and history stay in sync.

3. Use stock

When you use items (chairside, etc.), choose the item and quantity. The system issues stock using FIFO: it consumes the oldest batches first (by purchase date, or receipt date if purchase date is missing). One OUT movement is logged. If there isn’t enough total quantity, the action is blocked and you’ll see how much is available.

4. Stock view

Shows total quantity per item. Open Batches to see each line’s quantity, expiry, and batch number—useful for expiry checks and audits.

5. Movement history

Every IN and OUT appears here with item, type, quantity, date, and reference (e.g. purchase, manual). Filter by item and date range to investigate usage.

6. Report

See how many items you run, low-stock items, and batches expiring in the next 30 days (only batches with an expiry date in that window).

Transactions — Dashboard Transactions — Billing Transactions — Payments Transactions — Expenses Transactions — Log

This screen summarizes your financial position from the unified transactions table: total money in, total out, net balance (profit & loss), and how much is still owed on open bills.

Use Period and From / To to choose a date range (presets or custom). KPI totals, the monthly chart, payment-method breakdown, and expense-by-category chart all use that same range. Pending payments is always the current unpaid total on open bills. Hover charts for values.

Create an invoice for a patient (and optional appointment), add line items, apply a discount, and save. The bill starts as pending until payments cover the final amount.

Use PDF on any row to download a printable invoice with your organisation branding (header/footer/seal when configured), line items, totals, and amount paid so far.

Record money received against a bill. Each payment creates an income entry in the ledger and updates the bill to partial or paid when the total is reached.

You cannot pay more than the remaining balance on a bill.

Log any outgoing cost (salary, rent, supplies, etc.). Each expense is recorded in the ledger as an expense line.

Optionally link an inventory purchase: the same amount creates stock (IN movement), ties the expense to that stock row, and keeps inventory and accounting in sync.

The transactions log is the single audit trail: every income (e.g. patient payments) and every expense, with category, method, date, and reference to the source record.

Use filters to review activity by type, category text, or date range.
